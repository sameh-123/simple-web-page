Great Mathematicians

Friedrich Gauss

Gauss, a renowned German mathematician, made significant contributions to number theory, algebra, and differential geometry, leaving a lasting impact on the field of mathematics.

Leonhard Euler

Euler was an influential 18th-century Swiss mathematician known for his significant contributions to various branches of mathematics, including calculus, graph theory, and number theory.

Blaise Pascal

Blaise Pascal was a 17th-century French mathematician, physicist, inventor, and philosopher known for Pascal's triangle, the Pascaline calculator, and contributions to fluid dynamics and probability theory.
